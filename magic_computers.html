Computers are Magic

by Tom

I'm in the middle of the south-western Utah dessert, just south of Zion National Park, and after waking up and chugging a liter of water, I walked around a bit trying to figure out what I wanted to do. I can't really watch youtube or browse the internet because my phone is always so close to dead; I didn't know what to do.

Then I remembered my trusty laptop! I scurried around, trying to find some shade to sit in, and (after failing to find any) opened her up and waited . The bootscreen is a faded gray, and in the glare I couldn't tell if it was working. I hesitated a moment, left clicked, and lo! The wonderful world of pixels had awakened before me. Like arriving in bed after a long journey, like setting foot on land after a voyage through the sea, the comfort and the stability of my sweet digital landscape aroused an awe for this machine that I hadn't felt since I was a kid.

I think of the enthusiasm and the excitement I had for computers and electronics when I was growing up. Apple was making inspirational product videos, with sexy, dramatic animations of this new slab that you could touch and interact with-- that you could do anything on! It's so damn exciting, there's so much charisma! But the appeal wasn't just the drama and flashyness.

The appeal came from the charisma of the creators, the masters of the magic. It wasn't just about beholding novel manipulations of atoms-- it was about acheiving that god-hood for myself. The careful planning the "visionary" design. (a href='visionary_apples.html' src='Apple liked that word')

The commercials would show some amazing person, reading with a cup of tea in a well-designed home, or taking photos and tracking storms on a glacier. I was fantasizing about adventure, and using magic to make it happen.

Now, I'm pretty anti-apple, but I must acknowledge the role they played in bringing cool technology to my life in the first place. The intuitive layout of iMovie 7 (the one that shipped with OSX Tiger in like 2007) allowed me to make some cool home-videos. That in turn gave me confidence in my ability to use these tools. Then self-identification as a tech-savy person sent me googling things, learning html for the first time, and trying to program.

Now, I use Linix (which might perfectly antithesize apple's UI strategy) because the magic feels accesible. It seems more like engineering, because I know that I can access all the machinery, even if I don't bother most of the time.

Magic Incantations

My first Computer Science Proffessor, Dr. Jeanna Mathews, used to call the headers to c++ files "Magic incantations" that you just need to say, without understanding them, so that the rest of the program will work. There is of course a lot of interesting machinery behind the seeming incantations, but without understanding the context (all the things we were learning in the programs) it wouldn't have been useful.

I still chant incantations a lot when I use Linux, but unlike with Apple, I can expect to someday understand the underlying machinery.
